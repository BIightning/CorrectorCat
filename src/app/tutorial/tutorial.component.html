Welcome to Corrector Cat!

My name is Corrector Cat and I am happy that you’re joining me on my adventure.
Let’s sink in into the world of stories. We will read and listen to different stories.
But the reader makes many mistakes.

Help me to correct him, so he can become a purrrfect reader as well.

First things first

We need to show him what kind of mistake he has made.
Otherwise he can’t improve his reading skills.
Therefore, I am going to show you errors which are made while reading.

Semantic errors

A semantic error is made if the spoken word does not match with the written word.
This could change the context of the story.

Example

Given text: “The house is burning.”
Correct audio

Given text: “The house is burning.”

Semantic error: “The horse is burning”

Wrong audio

Syntactic Error

A syntactic error is made if the spoken word does not match with the sentence structure.
This could change the content of the story.

Example

Given text: “The house is burning.”
Correct audio

Given Text: “The telephone is ringing”

Syntactic error: “The telephone is rigging”

Wrong audio

Context Error

The way the words are spoken do not match with the context of the story.
(e.g. by reading with an unsuitable emotion)

Example

Given text: “The house is burning.”
Correct audio

Given Text: “ I am terrified” (saying it in a terrified way)

Context error: “ I am terrified (saying it in a happy way)

Wrong audio

Phrasing error

Reads in monotone with little sense of phrase boundaries; frequently reads word-by-word

Example

Given text: “The house is burning.”
Correct audio

Given Text: “I am excited”

Phrasing error: The sentence is read without excitement

Wrong audio

Smoothness error

Reads words as if simply to get them out.
Makes frequent extended pauses, hesitations, false starts, sound outs, repetitions, and/ or multiple attends

Example

Given text: “The house is burning.”
Correct audio

Given text: “They watch TV in the living room”

Smoothness error: The word “living room” is not read fluently

Wrong audio

Tense Error

The spoken word is read out in a wrong tense, which could change the content of the story.

Example

- Given text: “Reading is fun”
Correct audio

Given text: “Reading is fun”

Tense error “Reading was fun”

Wrong audio
